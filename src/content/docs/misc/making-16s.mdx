## On Difficulty

In base-game XDRV, the difficulty scale goes from 1 to 15. There are no official 16s in EX-XDRiVER (though there will probably be one in the future). It might sound like there is a lot of untread ground with 16s, which is true, but its also a double edged sword: **you can do a lot of experimenting with 16s, but it's also really easy to make a 16 that just isn't fun.**

To make a 16 difficulty chart that feels fair, balanced, and fun, you need to have a really strong understanding of XDRV's mechanics and chart style. For many charters, this understanding only comes with time.

Lastly, 16s are so difficult that the strenuousness of the charts will always mask the quality of the chart a little bit. In short, a good 16 chart will typically feel a bit poorer than a good 15 chart, largely because pushing difficulty has become the focus.

<Aside type="danger" title="Tbh">

If you make a 16 as your first chart, it probably won't be that good.

</Aside>

## On Accessibility

A very small portion of players can even play 16s (and none of them can really play 16s "comfortably"--they're a stretch for everyone). This is for a few reasons:

- EX-XDRiVER has no base-game EX16s.
- EX-XDRiVER's 15 range is very wide, spanning charts of considerably different intensities.
- EX-XDRiVER is hard, and the playerbase is relatively small.
  - The customs playerbase for EX-XDRiVER is even smaller.

As a reference, check the [Overall Rating leaderboards](https://steamcommunity.com/stats/2636020/leaderboards/12011807/) for EX-XDRiVER. Consider that only players with 14.000 rating or higher (written as 14,000 on the Steam leaderboards) can reasonably challenge an EX16 chart. As of writing this, there are only *13 players* that meet that threshold.

Long story short, if you want your chart to be accessible to more players, making a 16 inherently runs counter to that.

<Aside type="caution" title="What about lowdiffs?">

The obvious workaround to this problem would be to make a lowdiff set for your chart. I actually have no qualms with that approach, but *realistically*, lowdiff chart sets are a lofty goal in of themselves.

You could also make a lowdiff set for a non-EX-16, and by having less of a difficulty span to cover, your final set would still probably be more accessible.

</Aside>

## On Screwing Around

A lot of charters use 16s as an opportunity to screw around. I don't want to get in the way of charters having fun; making a mix of serious and unserious charts is a great way to stay engaged with charting as a medium.

With that said, "hard for the sake of being hard" isn't the most engaging angle for an XDRV chart, even as a joke. Additionally, if your *first* chart is a joke 16, the community might expect the wrong things from you as a charter.

## The Bait and Switch

I think a lot of the things I said about 16s actually apply to 14s and 15s as well. Even these difficulties of charts are less accessible to the larger playerbase. Speaking from experience, if you make charts of difficulties that more players can comfortably play (typically within the lower HY range), you will get more genuine feedback and more engagement overall.
